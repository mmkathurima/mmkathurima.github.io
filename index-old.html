Spare Parts Catalogue Management System

This is an e-commerce system dedicated to spare parts.
It allows for:
  1. Consumers to check spare part prices without the added overhead costs.
  2. Consumers to look up locations of the nearest spare part retail shops where they can order spare parts at cheaper prices. This improves accessibility to spare part sellers.
  3. Consumers to place items into virtual shopping carts and order them.
  4. Spare part dealers to catalogue spare parts for sale through electronic means which is more efficient, faster and takes up significantly less space. Spare parts can be categorized into body parts, make and model in which they belong to.
  5. Spare part dealers to manage their inventory.

Student Cafeteria Inventory Management System

An information system which allows an employee to log and manage levels of food items stock in the school cafeteria. The system streamlines the process of logging in such a way that it is no longer manual and also automates stock management so that a system administrator is informed when they have low quantities of goods.
It allows for:
  1. Reduce human errors including duplication, truncation, omission and transposition, caused by poor record keeping which in turn leads to inconsistencies in records and losses whereby there is overestimation in the quantity of goods and there is the challenge of understocking after the aforementioned overestimation. This ends up hurting the business once the business owner later realizes that the stock available is insufficient to continue with the day's ongoings. This can be achieved through the system itself raising an alert if an employee tries to enter duplicate records.
  2. Allow for easy entry, modification, removal, retrieval and generation of inventory records. Data can be easily entered into the system from where they can be updated, deleted, accessed and viewed. Receipts and record listings can be exported to the file system.
  3. Solve the challenges of inefficiency that affect the student cafeteria clientele by assisting to ensure that records are added to the system and are updated frequently. This can be done by the system adjusting its stock levels automatically each time items are destocked or a sale is made. Regular updating of records i.e., the synchronization of orders and sales will enable a business owner to keep track of items coming in and leaving the storage space in my case the consumption of food stuffs. This also keeps them in the know of whether an item is in stock or not.
  4. Enhance productivity among employees since the mundane tasks will be done for them by the system therefore leaving them with the more productive tasks.
  5. Another objective accomplishable by the system is to allow a business owner to be more in the know of all goods in their inventory such that they can easily spot which goods are more in demand hence they can stock up on more of that item. This helps in sales analysis where the business owner can stock less and less on goods that have low demand. This can be attained by the system’s ability to print receipts on sales then comparisons can be further made.
  6. This system will also allow for growth of the organization. The business owner can venture out and expand their business without the worry of stock management. The school cafeteria management system can allow for large amounts of data to be input and safely stored at one point.
